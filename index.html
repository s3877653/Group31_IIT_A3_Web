Overview:

Topic:

With the rapid development of the internet, it is obvious that an enormous amount of data is collected, consumed or delivered daily. Due to this reason, many tech companies have started developing servers to be easier to handle data. However, as the amount of data is increasing, companies are forced to have further upgrades on the servers, which requires a large amount of money. Therefore, they started developing cloud storage, which also has high storage capacity but offers lower cost of maintenance. Cloud storage is implemented not only for businesses, but also for individuals to increase their daily productivity. Realizing the great potential of cloud storage, our team also decided to build a cloud storage application ourselves.

Our cloud storage should be a representation of fast adaptation since the IT world is evolving rapidly. It will support both offline and online environments, multiple operating systems from mobile devices such as IOS, Android to desktops devices like MacOs, Windows, etc… Moreover, our application will categorize data and allow people to adjust their own setting, helping the storage to be more personalized. In short, our storage will serve small businesses and individuals with better personalization compared to other companies.

Landscape:

There are many popular companies which have already developed this type of technology such as Apple with iCloud, Google with Drive, Microsoft with Onedrive, etc… However, these cloud storage services require the internet in order to have further access to the data in the storage. With many people, this is still fine since the internet is all over the world right now. However, we think this still consists of inadequacies because many countries with low incomes or with high internet costs would not be able to sustain the internet daily or monthly. Therefore, we have decided that our cloud storage will be able to utilise both offline and online environments. It can store data offline and when there is an internet connection, it will automatically synchronise the data to the customers’ storage accounts. This is the main difference between our project and other competitors, and this will be further discussed in the detailed description.

Testing:

-Some people think that the developers know their product the most, just like how a mother knows her baby best. However just because they created the application does not mean they know it the best. This is because they have biases and most importantly the developers are not your users. This is why we need to test our products.

-There are 2 types of testing that we are planning to do, user testing which involves users and server testing which does not require any participant. Before we come up with a fully completed, refined product we need to do testing. First we will need to create a mockup of our project, after that we will use tools like paper.io or online prototype tools to create our first prototype. Our first prototype will be quick and rough, this means that it will not look well polished like the final product but it will have key features that we want in our final product. Now we need to look for our users, we should apply design thinking here. We need to know what do our users want from our application, why are they using it, what are they using it for. The next step after that is making a persona, a profile, a user that will use our application. The information in this persona can be their age group, profession, reason they are using it, their knowledge on software and other soft skills that they have. For example, if our users are mostly college students we can look for users, participants in RMIT or other colleges. The user testing phase began when we finished our prototype, a form asking participants to give us the right to own and share their information, a list of tasks to give to users and an interview question and answer form.

-To test user experience first we will ask them the right to own information they have given, this will include their age, gender, name, ethnicity, skills navigating new software and using new technology. After they have agreed to participate, we will begin to give them a list of tasks that we want the users to do. Beforehand these goals will be broken down to small step by step tasks for us to compare with what step the user is doing. This will help us see which step troubles the user the most and how intuitive our application is for new users. The users will also be timed because speed is also a very important factor when determining how successful our product is. Afterward there will be one final question and answer interview to ask for more information like what the users found useful, bad, hard to understand, what they think about the app, what they think will improve our product.

-The server testing will be conducted after our final product is finished. We will launch a test server to see if our server is online and working. We will test if we can store anything in the server database. We will also check up with an IT security expert to see if our server protections, like firewall for example, are strong enough to fend off cyber attacks.
